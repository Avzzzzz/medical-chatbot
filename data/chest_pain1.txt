Fever is a temporary rise in body temperature, often due to an illness. It is a natural response of the immune system to fight off infection. A normal body temperature is typically around 98.6°F (37°C). Fever is usually considered significant when body temperature rises above 100.4°F (38°C).

What causes fever?
- Viral infections (e.g., flu, COVID-19, dengue)
- Bacterial infections (e.g., strep throat, typhoid)
- Heat exhaustion or heat stroke
- Autoimmune disorders
- Certain medications or vaccines

When should you see a doctor?
- If the fever lasts more than 2–3 days
- If the temperature exceeds 103°F (39.4°C)
- If it's accompanied by symptoms like rash, stiff neck, confusion, vomiting, or difficulty breathing
- For infants under 3 months with a fever over 100.4°F (38°C)

How is fever treated?
- Most fevers resolve on their own
- Over-the-counter medications like paracetamol or ibuprofen can reduce fever
- Stay hydrated and rest
- Seek medical help for persistent or high-grade fevers

Which specialist should you consult?
- General Physician or Family Doctor (for most fevers)
- Pediatrician (for children)
