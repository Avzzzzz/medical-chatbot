Headache is a common medical symptom that can be caused by a variety of conditions.

What causes headache?
- Muscle Tension: Tension headaches occur when muscles in the neck and scalp become tense or contract.
- Stress: Stress is a major trigger for tension headaches.
- Anxiety and Depression: These mental health conditions can also contribute to tension headaches.
- Physical Factors: Lack of exercise, poor posture, and eyestrain can also trigger tension headaches.
- Sleep Disturbances: Lack of sleep or changes in sleep patterns can trigger headaches.
- Dehydration: Dehydration can disrupt blood flow to the brain, leading to headaches.
- Caffeine Withdrawal: Regular caffeine consumption can lead to headaches when caffeine is withdrawn.
- Diet: Skipping meals, consuming alcohol, or certain foods can trigger headaches.


When should you see a doctor?
- If headache is persistent, severe, or worsening
- If it is accompanied by other symptoms like fever, fatigue, or bleeding
- If it interferes with daily life or causes concern

How is headache treated?
- Treatment depends on the underlying cause
- Over-the-counter medications, lifestyle changes, or prescription treatment may be used

Which specialist should you consult?
- Neurologist
