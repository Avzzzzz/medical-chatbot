Vomiting is a common medical symptom that can be caused by a variety of conditions.

What causes vomiting?
- Gastrointestinal (GI) conditions like peptic ulcers, gastritis and gastroparesis.
- Seasickness and other motion sicknesses.
- Early pregnancy.
- Intense pain.
- Food poisoning.
- Indigestion (nausea after eating).
- Infections.
- Migraine headaches.
- Vertigo.
- Certain smells or odors.
- Overindulgence of alcohol or marijuana.
- Medications (for example, as a side effect of chemotherapy).

When should you see a doctor?
- If vomiting is persistent, severe, or worsening
- If it is accompanied by other symptoms like fever, fatigue, or bleeding
- If it interferes with daily life or causes concern

How is vomiting treated?
- Treatment depends on the underlying cause
- Over-the-counter medications, lifestyle changes, or prescription treatment may be used

Which specialist should you consult?
- Gastroenterologist
