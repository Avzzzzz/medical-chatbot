Autism is a common medical symptom that can be caused by a variety of conditions.

What causes autism?
- Heritability: Autism tends to run in families. If one child has autism, there is an increased risk for siblings.
- Gene mutations: Some rare mutations or changes in genes (like CHD8, SHANK3, SCN2A) have been linked to autism.
- Chromosomal abnormalities: Conditions like Fragile X syndrome and Rett syndrome are associated with ASD.
- Prenatal exposures:Infections during pregnancy (e.g., rubella, cytomegalovirus),Exposure to harmful chemicals or drugs (e.g., valproic acid, thalidomide),Complications like preterm birth or low birth weight,Advanced parental age (especially paternal).

When should you see a doctor?
- If autism symptoms is persistent, severe, or worsening
- If it is accompanied by other symptoms like fever, fatigue, or bleeding
- If it interferes with daily life or causes concern

How is autism symptoms treated?
- Treatment depends on the underlying cause
- Over-the-counter medications, lifestyle changes, or prescription treatment may be used

Which specialist should you consult?
- Child Psychiatrist
