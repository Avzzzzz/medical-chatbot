Constipation is a common medical symptom that can be caused by a variety of conditions.

What causes constipation?
- Not eating enough fiber.
- Not drinking enough water (dehydration).
- Not getting enough exercise.
- Consuming large amounts of milk or cheese.
- Stress.
- Strong pain medicines, like narcotics containing codeine, oxycodone (Oxycontin®) and hydromorphone (Dilaudid®).
- Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs), like ibuprofen (Advil®, Motrin®) and naproxen (Aleve®).

When should you see a doctor?
- If constipation is persistent, severe, or worsening
- If it is accompanied by other symptoms like fever, fatigue, or bleeding
- If it interferes with daily life or causes concern

How is constipation treated?
- Treatment depends on the underlying cause
- Over-the-counter medications, lifestyle changes, or prescription treatment may be used

Which specialist should you consult?
- Gastroenterologist
