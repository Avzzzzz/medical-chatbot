Bone fracture is a common medical symptom that can be caused by a variety of conditions.

What causes bone fracture?
- Fall from a height
- Trauma
- Motor vehicle accidents
- Repetitive forces, such as those caused by running, can cause stress fractures of the foot, ankle, tibia, or hip.

When should you see a doctor?
- If bone fracture is persistent, severe, or worsening
- If it is accompanied by other symptoms like fever, fatigue, or bleeding
- If it interferes with daily life or causes concern

How is bone fracture treated?
- Treatment depends on the underlying cause
- Over-the-counter medications, lifestyle changes, or prescription treatment may be used

Which specialist should you consult?
- Orthopedic
