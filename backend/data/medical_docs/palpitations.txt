Palpitations is a common medical symptom that can be caused by a variety of conditions.

What causes palpitations?
- Emotions, such as anxiety, stress, fear and panic.
- Exercise.
- Pregnancy.
- Caffeine.
- Overactive thyroid.
- Low levels of sugar, potassium or oxygen.
- Low carbon dioxide in your blood.
- Fever.

When should you see a doctor?
- If palpitations is persistent, severe, or worsening
- If it is accompanied by other symptoms like fever, fatigue, or bleeding
- If it interferes with daily life or causes concern

How is palpitations treated?
- Treatment depends on the underlying cause
- Over-the-counter medications, lifestyle changes, or prescription treatment may be used

Which specialist should you consult?
- Cardiologist
