Cold and cough is a common medical symptom that can be caused by a variety of conditions.

What causes cold and cough?
- Runny nose
- Sneeze
- Fever of 101 to 102 degrees Fahrenheit (38.3 to 38.9 degrees Celsius).
- Increased drooling because of sore throat and difficulty swallowing.

When should you see a doctor?
- If cold and cough is persistent, severe, or worsening
- If it is accompanied by other symptoms like fever, fatigue, or bleeding
- If it interferes with daily life or causes concern

How is cold and cough treated?
- Treatment depends on the underlying cause
- Over-the-counter medications, lifestyle changes, or prescription treatment may be used

Which specialist should you consult?
- General Physician
