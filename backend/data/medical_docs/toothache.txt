Toothache is a common medical symptom that can be caused by a variety of conditions.

What causes toothache?
- Cavities.
- Abscessed tooth.
- Cracked tooth.
- A damaged dental restoration (like a filling or crown).
- Teeth grinding or clenching (bruxism).
- Gum disease.

When should you see a doctor?
- If toothache is persistent, severe, or worsening
- If it is accompanied by other symptoms like fever, fatigue, or bleeding
- If it interferes with daily life or causes concern

How is toothache treated?
- Treatment depends on the underlying cause
- Over-the-counter medications, lifestyle changes, or prescription treatment may be used

Which specialist should you consult?
- Dentist
