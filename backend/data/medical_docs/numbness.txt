Numbness is a common medical symptom that can be caused by a variety of conditions.

What causes numbness?
- Collapsed backbone due to osteoporosis.
- Peripheral neuropathy.
- Nerve compression syndromes.
- Neuropathic pain.
- Ruptured or herniated disk.
- Carpal tunnel syndrome.
- Diabetes.
- Infections such as shingles, HIV or Lyme disease.
- Kidney disease.
- Multiple sclerosis (MS).
- Stroke.
- Arthritis.

When should you see a doctor?
- If numbness is persistent, severe, or worsening
- If it is accompanied by other symptoms like fever, fatigue, or bleeding
- If it interferes with daily life or causes concern

How is numbness treated?
- Treatment depends on the underlying cause
- Over-the-counter medications, lifestyle changes, or prescription treatment may be used

Which specialist should you consult?
- Neurologist
